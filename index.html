Under the Ocean

In the endless sway of the salty tide,
Tiny architects of the ocean hide.
They cement their heads to the wooden post,
And cling to the hulls of the ships they ghost.
With feathered fans in the rolling sea,
They stretch their limbs in the mystery.
To seek the truth of this anchored crust,
To find what lies under my crust.
